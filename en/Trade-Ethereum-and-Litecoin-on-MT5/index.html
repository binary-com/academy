What is a cryptocurrency

A cryptocurrency is a type of digital currency that relies on cryptography techniques and a variation of the blockchain technology (a kind of ‘public ledger’) to secure its transactions.

Cryptocurrencies are:

  • Anonymous – Transactions can take place without any exchange of personal information
  • Decentralised – No government agency or bank controls it, and there can be no central point of failure. Instead, it’s maintained by a peer-to-peer network
  • Almost impossible to counterfeit – The technology that powers cryptocurrencies record each transaction. Several copies of the transaction are available, and can be verified by anyone

Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Litecoin are some of the most popular cryptocurrencies around.
